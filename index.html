A paraphrase expresses the same meaning as the original text but uses different words (Oshima & Hogue, cited in Bretag, Crossman & Bordia 2007).

Quoting Fahey (cited in Frost 2008, p. 69) provides evidence that ‘wages for unskilled work remained lower than those in the capital cities’.

OR

The value placed on private property by pastoralists ‘clashed with the nomadic, customary nature of Aboriginal society and this discord often erupted in violence’ (Reynolds, cited in Frost 2008, p. 63).

NB: The year of the secondary source is not cited.

For books, book chapters, journals, or periodicals, the information should be in the following order: 1. author(s) family name followed by initials, or editor(s) family name for edited book followed by (ed. or eds) 2. year of publication 3. title of article (in journal) or chapter (in book) in single quotation marks 4. editor’s family name (for chapter in an edited book) 5. title of the book, journal or periodical in italics with subtitle (if any) edition (for book, if other than the first edition) or volume and issue numbers (for journal) 6. name of publisher (for book) 7. place of publication (for book) 8. page number(s) (for book chapter or journal article)

For electronic documents the information should be in the following order: 1. author(s) family name, editor(s) or sponsoring organisation 2. title of the page or online journal article 3. title of the site (go to the site's homepage) or online journal 4. date the page was last updated, or the copyright date 5. type of medium (e.g. eBook, motion film, figure) 6. date you viewed the page 7. the full internet address URL (uniform resource locator) of the page in angled brackets i.e. <http://www.etc> 8. or the DOI 9. or the distributor (for CD-ROMs and networked databases)

When referring to more than one work by the same author at one point in the text, if the works have been published in different years, arrange the citation in chronological order by date of publication, beginning with the earliest.

(Cottrell 2005, 2008)

When quoting, place a semi-colon after a page number reference to separate it from the date that follows.

(Cottrell 2005, p. 5; 2008, p. 11)

If the works have been published in the same year, a lower case letter of the alphabet is attached to the publication date. The order is established on the letter-by-letter alphabetical order of the titles. For example, two titles by the author Blainey, Black kettle and full moon: daily life in a vanished Australia and A game of our own: the origins of football, would be cited as 2003a and 2003b respectively.

When citing, separate the citations with a comma.

(Blainey 2003a, 2003b)

When quoting from works published in the same year by an author, separate the citations with a semi-colon after the page number.

(Blainey 2003a, p. 10; 2003b, p. 15)
